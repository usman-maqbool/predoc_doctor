Security and Privacy

Patient answers are for the doctor only. All information is encrypted and stored on secure computers which are protected by multiple barriers, both physical and digital. These barriers include extensive password systems, authorisation and physical barriers like professional security guards and CCTV. Even if there were to be a breach of data, identifying information like name and date of birth have been removed and anonymised. Patient answers are reviewed by PreDoc staf to improve the quality of PreDoc and patient care.

Compliances

The Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) of Australia set regulations about medical software. PreDoc does not currently provide a diagnosis or advice, it simply collects information from patients and summarises it for doctors. PreDoc automates the workfow of doctors collecting information from patients and as such is not treated as a medical device. PreDoc complies with the Privacy Act 1988.
